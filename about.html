OUR STORY

About the Cathedral

✦

The mother church of the Diocese of Ekiti — a living witness to God's faithfulness across generations.

SINCE 1929

A Century of
Faith & Worship

✦

The history of St. Patrick's Catholic Cathedral is intertwined with the very beginning of Catholicism in the Ekiti heartland. Evangelisation in this region began under the aegis of Bishop O'Rourke of the Society of African Missions (SMA), who succeeded Bishop Terrien in 1929 when the area was part of the ancient Bight of Benin mission.

The church building — originally known as St. George's Church — was established by the Irish SMA fathers who brought the Catholic faith to the people of Ado-Ekiti. When the Irish missionaries arrived and planted deep roots, it was renamed St. Patrick's, in honour of the patron saint of Ireland, a fitting tribute to those who crossed oceans to share the Gospel.

On July 30, 1972, the Diocese of Ado-Ekiti was created from the Diocese of Ondo. Just months later, on December 11, 1972, it was officially renamed the Diocese of Ekiti. St. Patrick's was elevated as its Cathedral Church — the Seat of the Bishop — a role it has held with dignity and grace ever since.

KEY MILESTONES

Our Timeline

✦
1929

Evangelisation Begins

Irish SMA missionaries arrive in Ado-Ekiti under Bishop O'Rourke, planting the seed of Catholic faith in the Ekiti heartland.

1930s–1960s

Growth of St. George's Church

The parish grows steadily under Irish missionary leadership. The church building expands and schools are established across Ekiti.

Late 1960s

Renamed St. Patrick's

Honouring Ireland's patron saint and the Irish missionaries who built the faith community, the church is renamed St. Patrick's.

July 30, 1972

Diocese of Ado-Ekiti Established

Carved out from the Diocese of Ondo, the new Diocese of Ado-Ekiti is formally erected, with St. Patrick's at its heart.

December 11, 1972

Renamed Diocese of Ekiti

The diocese is officially renamed the Diocese of Ekiti, becoming suffragan of Lagos. St. Patrick's is elevated as the Cathedral Church.

April 17, 2010

Bishop Felix Femi Ajakaye Installed

His Lordship Most Rev. Felix Femi Ajakaye is ordained and installed as the Bishop of Ekiti, continuing a legacy of faithful shepherding.

2026 & Beyond

Continuing the Mission

Today, St. Patrick's stands as a vibrant parish family of over 5,000 faithful, carrying the light of the Gospel into every corner of Ekiti State.

SHEPHERD OF THE FLOCK

Most Rev. Felix Femi Ajakaye

DD · BISHOP OF EKITI

✦

Bishop Felix Femi Ajakaye was ordained to the episcopate and installed as the fourth Bishop of Ekiti on April 17, 2010. A native of Ekiti State, his Lordship has dedicated his episcopal ministry to deepening the faith of the people, promoting education, and championing social justice across the diocese.

Under his leadership, the Diocese of Ekiti has witnessed remarkable growth in pastoral outreach, the establishment of new parishes, and a renewed commitment to youth evangelisation and Catholic education.
